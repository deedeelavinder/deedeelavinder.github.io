About Me

Applying myself to learn new things, and learn them well, is not a novel undertaking for me. Eight years ago, with no business or retail experience, I founded a company and opened a storefront. Determination and an ability to manage multiple objectives served me well. With systematized operations running smoothly, a well-developed staff and six awards, I was ready for a new challenge and sold the business this past summer. I have known for years that I wanted to work in software development and so, in September, I started the Rails Engineering course at The Iron Yard. For three months, with instruction and guidance from experienced developers, I put my life on hold and spent 80-90 hours a week coding and learning all I could about developing web applications using Ruby on Rails.

My decision to make a career pivot and pursue software development full-time is affirmed every time I write code; I want to channel my enthusiasm, tackle challenging problems and produce simple, elegant software solutions. With more than twenty years of work experience, I bring more to the table than just my newly acquired skills. I have an established history of team success and getting things done. I am passionate about programming and want to pursue the craft and life-long adventure of technology.
